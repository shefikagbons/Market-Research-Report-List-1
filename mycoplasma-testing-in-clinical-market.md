<p><p>Mycoplasma testing in the clinical market involves various methods to detect mycoplasma contamination in cell cultures and biological samples. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) offers high sensitivity and specificity for identifying mycoplasma DNA. ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay) provides a way to detect mycoplasma through antibody-antigen interactions. Enzymatic methods leverage enzyme reactions to indicate mycoplasma presence, while DNA staining techniques use fluorescent dyes to visualize mycoplasma DNA in samples. Each method has distinct advantages, catering to diverse testing needs.</p></p>
