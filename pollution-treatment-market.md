<p><ul><li>Point Source Pollution</li><li>Nonpoint Source Pollution</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pollution treatment market is categorized into two main types: point source pollution and nonpoint source pollution. Point source pollution refers to specific, identifiable sources, such as industrial outlets or wastewater treatment plants, which discharge pollutants directly into water bodies. In contrast, nonpoint source pollution arises from diffuse sources, such as agricultural runoff or urban stormwater, making it harder to trace and manage. Both types require distinct treatment solutions, creating varied market dynamics and regulatory approaches for pollution control.</p></p>
