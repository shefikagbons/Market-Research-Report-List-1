<p><p>The medical hydrogen generator market primarily consists of two types: electrolytic and chemical reaction-based generators. Electrolytic generators use electrical current to split water into hydrogen and oxygen, ensuring a high purity level for medical applications. In contrast, chemical reaction generators produce hydrogen through chemical reactions, typically involving metal hydrides or acidic solutions. Both types serve vital roles in medical settings, providing hydrogen for therapeutic uses such as inhalation therapy and supporting advancements in treatments for various diseases.</p></p>
