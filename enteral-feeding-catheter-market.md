<p><ul><li>Gastrostomy Tube</li><li>Nasoenteric Tube</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The enteral feeding catheter market primarily comprises three types: gastrostomy tubes, nasoenteric tubes, and others. Gastrostomy tubes are surgically inserted into the stomach for long-term feeding, ideal for patients unable to swallow. Nasoenteric tubes are placed through the nose into the small intestine, suitable for short-term use in patients with temporary feeding needs. The "other" category encompasses various alternative feeding devices, catering to specific patient requirements, including jejunostomy tubes and percutaneous feeding options.</p></p>
